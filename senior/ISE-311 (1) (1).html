  • Definition:

    Feedback from both hospital staff and patients has brought to light existing challenges in organizing information within our facility, particularly in the management of details concerning doctors, patients, drugs, and nurses. These difficulties have tangible effects on the efficiency of healthcare services and patient care. Recognizing the imperative for improvement, we are actively considering the implementation of a specially designed database system to address these specific concerns. The goal is to enhance the overall organization and accessibility of critical healthcare data. This potential solution aims to offer a tailored approach to more effectively manage data related to doctors, patients, drugs, and nurses. This, in turn, is expected to facilitate more efficient daily operations for hospital staff and improved access for patients, ultimately enhancing healthcare services and patient care.

    System Explanation and Description:

    • The hospital is structured into departments, each overseen by a doctor. Each department has a secretary, a phone number, and is associated with a specific doctor.
    • Doctors are crucial members of the hospital staff, identified by a unique Doctor ID. We store their SSN, full name (composed of Name and Surname), Birth Date, Gender, Phone Number, Salary, and Title. Each doctor is assigned to a specific department.
    • Patients are individuals receiving medical care at the hospital. Patient records include SSN, full name (Name and Surname), Birth Date, Gender, Phone Number, and Blood Type.
    • Nurses are essential members of the medical staff. Each nurse is identified by an ID and has associated attributes, including SSN, full name (Name and Surname), Birth Date, Gender, Phone Number and Salary.
    • The hospital maintains a Pharmacy, which provides medicines to patients. The Pharmacy is identified by its Phone Number and stores information about Medicine Prices and the Number of Medicines available.
    • Medicines, each identified by a unique Trade Number are sold at the Pharmacy. Each medicine also has additional detail in the form of it's formula.
    • The hospital also manages a Medical Supply Warehouse with a unique Warehouse ID, responsible for storing medical supplies. Each supply is identified by a unique Medical Supply ID, and information such as Expiration Date and amount are recorded.
    • Relationships exist between these entities, with departments being managed by doctors, nurses working in collaboration with doctors and patients being attended to by doctors. The Pharmacy supplies medicines to patients. The Medical Supply Warehouse provides necessary medical supplies to departments to support the hospital's operations.

Question 6.1: Write a SQL query to retrieve the full name and title of a doctor along with the full name of the nurse they work together with. Only include results where the doctor's full name is 'Dr. John Smith'. Order the results by the doctor's full name.

SELECT D.Full_Name AS Doctor_Name, D.Title AS Doctor_Title, N.Full_Name AS Nurse_Name
FROM DOCTORS D LEFT JOIN WORK_TOGETHER WT ON D.SSN = WT.Doctor_SSN
LEFT JOIN NURSES N ON WT.Nurse_SSN = N.SSN
WHERE D.Full_Name = 'Dr. John Smith'
ORDER BY D.Full_Name
